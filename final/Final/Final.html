Welcome

This site explores Hawaiian culture—a rich blend of history, language, environment, and traditions—to foster learning and appreciation for Hawaiʻi's heritage. Whether you are a student, a visitor, or simply curious, this hub offers accessible information and ways to connect with the culture.

Explore the pages above to learn about Hawaiian history, language, environment, and traditions.

History of Hawai‘i

Hawaiian history spans from ancient Polynesian settlement to the Kingdom era and the changes brought by U.S. annexation. Key historical figures shaped the islands’ trajectory, from kings to cultural leaders.

  • Polynesian Settlement (circa 400 AD)
  • Kingdom Period (1795–1893)
  • Annexation by the United States (1898)

Learn more about Hawaiian history through museums and historical societies dedicated to preserving this unique past.

The Hawaiian Language (‘Ōlelo Hawai‘i)

The Hawaiian language is experiencing a revival after near disappearance in the 20th century. Here are some common words:

  • Aloha – Hello / Goodbye / Love
  • Mahalo – Thank you
  • ʻOhana – Family

Audio resources and online dictionaries can help you with pronunciation and deeper learning.

Hawaiian Environment

Hawai‘i’s islands differ greatly in geography and environment. Below is a comparison table highlighting key features.

Comparison of Major Hawaiian Islands

Oʻahu Central Tropical/Urban Diamond Head Honolulu

Hawaiʻi (The Big Island) South Volcano Mauna Loa Hilo

Protecting the environment (mālama ʻāina) is a cultural value and vital for future generations.

Traditions & Festivities

Experience key Hawaiian traditions such as hula dance, lei-making, luaus, and the makahiki festival celebrating the harvest season. Annual cultural festivals provide vibrant opportunities to connect with the heritage.

Visit museums and cultural centers to learn more and participate in events.
