Foundations of Hawaiian Culture

Native Hawaiian culture formed over hundreds of years as Polynesian voyagers settled the islands and developed unique practices in agriculture, navigation, religion, and art.

Core values such as aloha (love, compassion), kuleana (responsibility), and mālama ʻāina emphasize deep relationships between people, ʻohana (family), and the ʻāina (land and sea).

Traditions and Practices

Important traditions include hula and mele (chant), which preserve history and story through movement and poetry, as well as lei-making, voyaging, and communal work in loʻi kalo (taro patches).

These practices continue today in both formal and community settings, showing how Hawaiian culture adapts while still honoring ancestral knowledge and spiritual connections to place.
