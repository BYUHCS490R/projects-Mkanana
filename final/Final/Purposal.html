Hawaiian Culture and Traditions - A Learning Hub

Why this topic? It highlights the unique and rich culture of Hawaiʻi, fostering understanding and respect for its history, language, environment, and traditions.

Content structure: Home: Introduction and site purpose.

History: Key periods and figures in Hawaiian history. Hawaiian Language: Revival, common words, and pronunciation resources. Environment (with table): Comparison of main islands by location, environment, features, and towns. Traditions & Festivities: Overview of cultural practices and major festivals. Contact: Form to share stories or questions.

Form fields: Name, Email, Interest in Hawaiian culture, Favorite tradition/word/place, Optional photo upload, Consent checkbox.

Data for table: Island name, Location, Environment type, Unique feature, Main town.

Content collection: Information and images sourced from reputable cultural institutions, published research, public domain media, and user contributions with permissions.

Planned references (at least 5 on site): Bishop Museum (https://www.bishopmuseum.org) ‘Ōlelo Hawai‘i Online (https://www.oleloonline.com) Hawaiian Tourism Authority National Park Service (Hawai‘i parks) University of Hawai‘i resources
